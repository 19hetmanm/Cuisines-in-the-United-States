History

Chinese people showed Japanese how to cultivate rice in 300 B.C. As well as showing the Japanese how to use chopsticks and introduced the usage of soy sauce and tofu in everyday life. In 700 A.D., meat consumption was prohibited by Buddhists so Buddhism introduced dishes made from raw fish and rice to Japanese people. Vegetarian dishes became popular in 1800’s. In 1200’s, trade between the different countries has become popular; Japanese cuisine mixed with Dutch and Portuguese. It incorporated potatoes, corn, and sweet potatoes and a frying technique called “tempura” became popular. During the 20th century, Japanese cuisine included bread, ice cream, coffee and other western foods.
